My Research

Meteors, or shooting stars, are constantly entering Earth's orbit and "burning up" in the atmosphere and rarely make it to the ground. This means that thousands of kilograms of meteoric material is deposited into the atmosphere daily, and the composition and specific amount is not well known. I use many different types of data sets, including optical cameras, radar measurements, and spectroscopy to determine the size, speed, and composition of the smallest meteors, down to only a few milligrams!
